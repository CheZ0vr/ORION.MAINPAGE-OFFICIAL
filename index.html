CHAPTER II

The First Light

Every fragrance begins
inside a laboratory.

ORION did not.

It began as a question.

What deserves to become a memory?

Not every scent
should remain
inside someone's life.

Only those
capable of becoming
part of their story.

The scent someone remembers after a goodbye.

The scent that quietly fills an empty elevator.

The scent still resting on yesterday's jacket.

The scent someone recognizes without seeing you.

Memory is invisible.
So is fragrance.

That connection
became our obsession.

Not projection.

Not longevity.

Not trends.

Memory.

Thousands of combinations
were imagined.

Most were forgotten.

Only a handful
deserved another trial.

Even fewer
deserved the ORION name.

Perfection is subtraction.

Every unnecessary note
was removed.

Every distraction
was erased.

Until only emotion
remained.

When someone wears ORION,
they are not borrowing
another identity.

They are amplifying
their own.

Fragrance should never
speak louder
than the person wearing it.

It should simply become
part of who they are.
